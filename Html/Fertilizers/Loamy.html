Silty Loam is a type of loamy soil that contains a higher proportion of silt particles. This composition enhances the soil's ability to retain nutrients and moisture, providing a more stable and fertile environment for plant growth compared to sandy loam. Silty loam has a smooth texture and holds together better than sandy soils, allowing for improved root penetration and growth.

Peaty Loam is a type of loamy soil that contains a significant amount of organic matter, particularly peat.
